\# DIGDUG.DO \& USDDD

\### A Permissionless Distribution Protocol and Performance-Driven Monetary System  

\*\*Whitepaper v0.1 (Living Document)\*\*



---



\## Abstract



DIGDUG.DO is a permissionless protocol that enables projects to distribute tokens to real users at scale, while giving participants a fair, engaging way to earn on-chain assets. It solves two persistent problems in crypto: ineffective token distribution and the absence of organic demand for native protocol currencies.



At the heart of DIGDUG.DO is \*\*USDDD\*\*, a native unit of account that begins as a utility and reward unit inside an active economy and is later formalized into a stable monetary instrument. USDDD is not launched as a stablecoin; it is \*\*grown into one\*\* through real usage, real demand, and disciplined monetary design.



---



\## 1. Problem Statement



\### 1.1 Token Distribution Is Broken



Projects attempting to grow token holder bases face systemic issues:

\- Airdrops are farmed by bots

\- Marketing spend lacks measurable on-chain outcomes

\- Holder counts are inflated and temporary

\- Distribution tools are opaque and centralized



\### 1.2 Stablecoins Are Launched Backwards



Most stablecoins:

\- Launch before usage exists

\- Rely on promised pegs

\- Attract speculative demand before real demand

\- Collapse when confidence breaks



DIGDUG.DO inverts this sequence.



---



\## 2. DIGDUG.DO Overview



DIGDUG.DO is a \*\*distribution protocol with a game-based interface\*\*.



It enables:

\- Participants to earn real tokens through engagement

\- Projects (sponsors) to acquire real holders with verifiable outcomes

\- A native economy where usage precedes monetization



The protocol is designed to be:

\- Permissionless

\- Self-serve

\- Transparent

\- Scalable without human intervention



---



\## 3. Participants (Retail Side)



\### 3.1 Who Participants Are



Participants are:

\- Airdrop hunters

\- Meme coin users

\- DeFi participants

\- Wallet-native users seeking fair incentives



They do not need to believe in a project.  

They need it to be \*\*real, free, and on-chain\*\*.



\### 3.2 Participant Flow



1\. Connect a wallet  

2\. Participate (“dig”)  

3\. Earn USDDD or sponsor tokens  

4\. Redeem, hold, or reuse rewards  



Participation is wallet-based and permissionless.



---



\## 4. Sponsors (Project Side)



\### 4.1 What Sponsors Want



Sponsors do not want “marketing”.  

They want \*\*measurable outcomes\*\*:



\- Guaranteed holders

\- Controlled token distribution

\- On-chain proof

\- Transparent reporting



\### 4.2 Sponsor Self-Serve Model



Any sponsor can:

1\. Connect a wallet  

2\. Define a campaign (token, amount, holder target, duration)  

3\. Deposit tokens into a protocol vault  

4\. Go live immediately  



No approvals.  

No negotiations.  

No admin intervention.



---



\## 5. Sponsor Pricing Philosophy (Freemium)



\### 5.1 Free Tier (Proof of Value)



The free tier exists to demonstrate effectiveness.



Limits include:

\- Small holder cap

\- Short runtime

\- Low per-wallet rewards

\- Shared relayer resources



Free proves \*\*functionality\*\*, not scale.



\### 5.2 Paid Upgrades (Fuel)



Sponsors may pay to:

\- Increase holder caps

\- Extend campaign duration

\- Boost visibility

\- Access faster settlement



Paid tiers amplify what already works.



---



\## 6. Anti-Abuse Architecture



DIGDUG.DO does not attempt to identify humans.  

It makes abuse \*\*uneconomical, slow, and unscalable\*\*.



\### 6.1 Defense-in-Depth



\- One wallet per claim per pool

\- Strict gas budgets

\- Time-based cooldowns

\- Rate-limited relayer queues

\- Voucher-based authorization

\- Behavior-based probability adjustment



Humans experience a smooth UX.  

Automation experiences friction.



---



\## 7. Protocol Architecture (High Level)



\- One global vault holds sponsor tokens  

\- One claim router authorizes payouts  

\- Multiple logical pools represent campaigns  

\- A relayer abstracts gas for participants  

\- A backend issues time-bound claim vouchers  



Contracts remain minimal and boring.  

Rules live at the protocol and system layer.

\## 8. USDDD Overview



USDDD is the native unit of account of DIGDUG.DO.



It is \*\*not launched as a stablecoin\*\*.  

It is introduced as a \*\*utility and reward unit\*\*.



---



\## 9. USDDD Monetary Philosophy



\### 9.1 Supply



\- Elastic

\- Performance-bound

\- No arbitrary minting

\- No fixed cap



Supply expands only when real economic activity exists.



\### 9.2 Issuance



\*\*Retail issuance\*\*

\- Earned through participation

\- Small, rate-limited, widely distributed



\*\*Institutional issuance\*\*

\- Introduced later

\- Deliberate and constrained

\- Supports liquidity and settlement



No individual or governance body has discretionary mint power.



\### 9.3 Burn



USDDD is burned when \*\*value is consumed\*\*, such as:

\- Unlocking actions

\- Boosting campaigns

\- Accessing scarce resources



Burns are permanent and utility-linked.
